# Route Economics

## Purpose

The route is the operational unit of mobile healthcare. Appointment-level profitability is necessary but insufficient because dispersed appointments can consume the margin created by individual visits.

## Core Equation

Route Contribution = Total Route Revenue - Appointment Direct Costs - Route-Specific Overhead

## Required Metrics

Track:

- Appointments per route
- Revenue per route
- Contribution per route
- Contribution margin percentage
- Total route miles
- Total route hours
- Drive minutes
- Clinical minutes
- Idle minutes
- Revenue per route hour
- Contribution per route hour
- Revenue per mile
- Contribution per mile
- Miles per appointment

## Density Principle

Density improves economics by spreading travel, dispatch, coordination, and route overhead across more revenue-producing encounters.

Compare routes using both absolute contribution and productivity. A route with higher revenue can be inferior if it requires disproportionate time and mileage.

## Route Quality Classification

### Strong
High contribution, high contribution per hour, and low miles per appointment.

### Productive but Fragile
Good contribution driven by premium pricing or one major client, but vulnerable to cancellation or concentration.

### Density Opportunity
Positive appointment economics but excessive travel or idle time. Improve scheduling, geographic clustering, or service windows.

### Margin Trap
Revenue appears attractive but route contribution per hour is weak after travel and overhead.

### Reject or Reprice
Negative contribution or economics that remain unattractive after realistic density improvements.

## Decision Questions

1. Can appointments be clustered geographically?
2. Can time windows be narrowed or standardized?
3. Can one anchor client create route density?
4. Is a mileage or travel surcharge required?
5. Should the route use a dedicated contractor, floating contractor, or employee?
6. What is the minimum appointments-per-route threshold?
7. What revenue per route hour is required to justify capacity?

## Strategic Rule

Do not optimize for the number of appointments completed. Optimize for economically productive access delivered per route hour.
